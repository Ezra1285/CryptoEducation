The Crypto Dictionary

A list the most common terms you will hear in crypto.

Bitcoin

The first and most valuable cryptocurrency, launched on Jan. 3, 2009. While its value has climbed steadily since then, it has seen wild fluctuations. In the past months alone, the price of Bitcoin has fluctuated from a record high of $60,000 to below $30,000.(1)

Bitcoin Cash

A peer-to-peer electronic cash system that formed from a fork of the original Bitcoin. Where Bitcoin is widely accepted as too volatile to be useful as a currency, Bitcoin Cash is designed to be better optimized for transactions. (1)

Blockchain

Blockchain, which consists of a series of blocks, is a digital ledger of all the verified transactions made on a particular cryptocurrency. (3)

Cryptocurrency

A type of currency that's digital and decentralized. Cryptocurrency can be used to buy and sell things, or as a long-term store of value. (1)

Decentralization

Decentralization refers to the property of a system in which nodes or actors work in concert in a distributed fashion to achieve a common goal.(2)

Ethereum

The second largest cryptocurrency by trade volume, Ethereum is a crypto network and software platform that developers can use to create new applications, and has an associated currency called ether. (1)

Exchange

Businesses that allow customers to trade cryptocurrencies for fiat money or other cryptocurrencies. (2)

Fork

A fork is when a cryptocurrency or blockchain-based network splits off into two distinct projects with their own code and set of governing principles. In the event of a “soft” fork, only one blockchain will remain operational, whereas “hard” forks result in two new brand chains. For example, Bitcoin Classic is a hard fork of Bitcoin, since both cryptocurrencies run on standalone blockchains, with their own sets of miners, nodes and network participants.(3)

Gas

Gas is the cost of computing power when completing transactions on the Ethereum blockchain, which gets passed on to network participants in the form of fees.(3)

Hash

A unique string of numbers and letters that identify blocks and are tied to crypto buyers and sellers. (1)

Mining

Mining is the process through which new cryptocurrencies are minted. Many cryptocurrencies depend on a proof-of-work mining system, whereby computational power is delegated towards solving complex math riddles as a way to simultaneously secure and power a network, while also minting new tokens. (3)

Market Capitalization (Market Cap)

Cryptocurrency market capitalization refers to the total value of all the coins that have been mined. You can calculate a crypto's market cap by multiplying the current number of coins by the current value of the coins.(1)

Node

The most basic unit of blockchain infrastructure that stores data. (2)

Peer-to-peer

The decentralized interactions between parties in a distributed network, partitioning tasks or workloads between peers.(2)

Public Key

A public key refers to a series of alphanumeric characters used to encrypt plain text messages into ciphertext. (2)

Private Key

A piece of code generated in asymmetric-key encryption process, paired with a public key, to be used in decrypting information hashed with the public key.(2)

Token

A token refers to a unit of a digital currency like Bitcoin. (3)

Algorithm

A process or set of rules to be followed in problem-solving or calculation operations, usually by a computer.(2)

Altcoin

Altcoin means all cryptocurrencies that aren't Bitcoin (and in some contexts, Ether). The phrase is used matter-of-factly to describe various other cryptocurrencies. (3)
