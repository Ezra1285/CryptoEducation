The benifits of crypto currency.

Why would I ever switch from my bank to crypto?

Property Traditional Banking Crypto Currency
Transactions All transactions must go through at least one middleman if not more before they can be processed and sent to the receiving party. Uses what is known as a peer-to-peer transaction system - meaning that transactions go directly from the sender to the receiver.
International Transfers First you must request that your bank wires the money to a certain bank and person in another county. Then this wire transfer can take on average 3-5 day and comes with an average fee of anywhere from 5%-9.3%. Can be sent to anywhere in the world with internet connection. In addition, transaction happen almost instantly and have an average fee of 1%.
Ownership Governed and completely controlled by a singular central authority. Is controlled by nobody and everybody. Everyone can access the network, but no one will ever be able to change it. All of the network and how it works is public data for anyone to see.
Security All information is stored on one or a few networks which can also be accessed by certain employees - meaning if one system was breached or an employee was compromised the whole system could be destroyed. Information is stored on the blockchain - a collection of nearly 70,000 individual systems each checking the other systems for errors. It would take over 51% of the computer being hacked simultaneously for the network to be affected.
Privacy Personal information is tied to each account and stored in the banks network. This information is usually used to confirm your identify when making certain purchases such as online ones. Bitcoin transactions are pseudonymous. This means that the transactions aren't completely anonymous, but that they can only be identified by a wallet address which can only be connected to a person if they make that information public.
Value legal tender whose value is tied to a government issued currency - like the U.S. dollar. A digital asset whose value comes from the utility of its native blockchain.
